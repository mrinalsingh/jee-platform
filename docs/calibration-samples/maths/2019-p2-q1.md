## Statement
Let
$$
P_1 = I = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix},\quad
P_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0\end{bmatrix},\quad
P_3 = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix},
$$
$$
P_4 = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix},\quad
P_5 = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0\end{bmatrix},\quad
P_6 = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix},
$$
and $\displaystyle X = \sum_{k=1}^{6} P_k \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & 0 & 2 \\ 3 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix} P_k^{T}$ where $P_k^{T}$ denotes the transpose of the matrix $P_k$.

Then which of the following options is/are correct?

- (A) If $X \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \alpha \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, then $\alpha = 30$
- (B) $X$ is a symmetric matrix
- (C) The sum of diagonal entries of $X$ is $18$
- (D) $X - 30I$ is an invertible matrix

## Official key
**Correct: (A), (B), (C). (D) is incorrect.**

## Derived solution (Claude â€” JEE-syllabus methods only)

### The IDEA
Each $P_k$ is a permutation matrix, so $P_k^{T} = P_k^{-1}$. The map $M \mapsto P_k M P_k^{T}$ permutes the rows AND columns of $M$ by the same permutation $\sigma_k \in S_3$. Summing over all of $S_3$ therefore averages $M$'s entries over the $S_3$-orbits in the index set $\{(i,j) : 1 \le i,j \le 3\}$, of which there are only two â€” the diagonal $\{(i,i)\}$ and the off-diagonal $\{(i,j) : i \ne j\}$.

### Working
Write $X_{ij} = \displaystyle\sum_{\sigma \in S_3} M_{\sigma(i),\, \sigma(j)}$ where $M$ is the given $3 \times 3$ matrix.

**Diagonal ($i = j$):** as $\sigma$ ranges over $S_3$, the value $\sigma(i)$ takes each of $1, 2, 3$ exactly $|S_3|/3 = 2$ times. So
$$X_{ii} = 2\,(M_{11} + M_{22} + M_{33}) = 2 \cdot (2 + 0 + 1) = 6.$$

**Off-diagonal ($i \ne j$):** as $\sigma$ ranges over $S_3$, the ordered pair $(\sigma(i), \sigma(j))$ ranges uniformly over the $6$ ordered pairs $(a,b)$ with $a \ne b$, each exactly once. So $X_{ij}$ equals the sum of off-diagonal entries of $M$:
$$X_{ij} = 1 + 3 + 1 + 2 + 3 + 2 = 12.$$

Hence $X = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 12 & 12 \\ 12 & 6 & 12 \\ 12 & 12 & 6\end{bmatrix}$.

### Verifying each option
- **(A)** $X \cdot [1,1,1]^{T} = [30, 30, 30]^{T} = 30 \cdot [1,1,1]^{T}$, so $\alpha = 30$. âś…
- **(B)** $X_{ij} = X_{ji}$ by construction (all diagonals equal, all off-diagonals equal). âś…
- **(C)** Trace $= 6 + 6 + 6 = 18$. âś…
- **(D)** From (A), $(X - 30I)\,[1,1,1]^{T} = 0$. A non-zero vector in the null space â‡’ $X - 30I$ is singular â‡’ not invertible. âťŚ
